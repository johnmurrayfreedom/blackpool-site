Universal Credit: A Complete Guide

Citizens Information & Advice Bureau | Last Updated: May 2025

What is Universal Credit?

Universal Credit is a payment to help with your living costs. It's paid monthly (or twice a month for some people in Scotland). Universal Credit has replaced the following benefits:

  • Child Tax Credit
  • Housing Benefit
  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Working Tax Credit

If you currently receive any of these benefits, you cannot claim Universal Credit at the same time. You will be migrated to Universal Credit eventually via a process called "managed migration."

Eligibility

You may be eligible for Universal Credit if:

  • You're on a low income or out of work
  • You're 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you're 16 to 17)
  • You're under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • You and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • You live in the UK

Your eligibility also depends on where you live, your immigration status, and whether you live with a partner.

Note: If you're in full-time education, you can only claim Universal Credit if certain conditions apply, such as being responsible for a child or having limited capability for work.

How Much You Can Get

Universal Credit consists of a standard allowance and any extra amounts that apply to you. These might include:

Standard Allowance (Monthly)

Your Circumstances Monthly Standard Allowance
Single and under 25 £311.68
Single and 25 or over £393.45
In a couple and both under 25 £489.23 (per couple)
In a couple and either of you is 25 or over £617.60 (per couple)

Extra Amounts

You may get more money on top of your standard allowance if you:

  • Have children
  • Have a disability or health condition which prevents you from working
  • Need help paying your rent
  • Care for a severely disabled person

Important: Your payment can be reduced due to earnings, other income, savings over £6,000, or other factors. Universal Credit is typically reduced by 55p for every £1 you earn.

How to Apply

You can apply for Universal Credit online at www.gov.uk/universal-credit. To complete your application, you'll need:

  • Your bank, building society or credit union account details
  • An email address
  • Your National Insurance number
  • Information about your housing, for example how much rent you pay
  • Details of your income, for example payslips
  • Details of savings and any investments, like shares or property
  • Details of how much you pay for childcare if you're applying for help with childcare costs

If you need help with your application, you can:

  • Call the Universal Credit helpline on 0800 328 5644
  • Book an appointment with our advisors
  • Use the Citizens Advice Help to Claim service by calling 0800 144 8 444

Managing Your Universal Credit Claim

Your Claimant Commitment

Your Universal Credit claim is based on a 'Claimant Commitment' which outlines what you've agreed to do to prepare for and look for work, or to increase your earnings if you're already working. Your commitment will depend on your circumstances.

Advance Payments

If you need help with your living costs while you wait for your first payment, you can apply for an advance. This is a loan that you must pay back from your future Universal Credit payments.

Reporting Changes

You must report any changes in your circumstances as they may affect your payment. This includes:

  • Finding a job or ending employment
  • Changes to your income or savings
  • Moving home
  • Changes to your household (e.g., having a baby, a partner moving in)

If You Disagree with a Decision

If you disagree with a decision about your Universal Credit, you can ask for the decision to be looked at again. This is called a 'mandatory reconsideration'. You must request this within one month of the date on your decision letter.

If you're still unhappy after the mandatory reconsideration, you can appeal to an independent tribunal. Our advisors can help you with this process.
